Cultural Appropriation

Cultural appropriation is the term used to describe the usage of items or aspects from a non-dominant culture in a way that supports oppression or perpetuates stereotypes while also disregarding its creator. It also involves the unlicensed use of aspects of their culture, such as their dress and dancing. When someone from one culture adopts clothing, iconography, trends, or styles from another culture that is not their own, this is known as cultural appropriation, sometimes known as cultural misappropriation. When a historically oppressed group's culture is being appropriated, some of the practice's most damaging instances occur.

Elements of Cultural Appropriation

Culture

Anything connected to a group of people based on their ethnicity, religion, geography, or social environment is referred to as their culture. The term "culture" refers to the beliefs, customs, language, artifacts, behaviors, conventions, values, or institutions of a particular ethnic group.

Cultural Designation

When someone adopts a characteristic from another culture with the intent of demeaning or demeaning members of that society, this is known as cultural denigration.

Cultural Apppreciation & Respect

Cultural appreciation entails the respectful adoption of components from another culture to exchange ideas and broaden one's perspective. Cultural appreciation can lead to tremendous respect and understanding between civilizations and innovative hybrids that combine different cultures.

How to avoid Cultural Appropriation?

Before you "borrow" from a culture, remember these questions:

  • Am I using something sacred to another culture?
  • Do my actions support stereotypes?
  • Am I neglecting to credit the source of my inspiration?

    • You will probably be able to prevent cultural appropriation if you can confidently respond "no" to each of those questions.
