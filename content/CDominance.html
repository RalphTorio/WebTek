Cultural Dominance

Cultural dominance is the use of elements of a dominant culture by members of a subordinated culture in a context in which the dominant culture has been imposed onto the subordinated culture, including appropriations that enact resistance.

Dominance of culture that is preserved over a specific period of time. This preservation of dominant culture may be influenced by social and governmental systems, where people with strong influence and authority significantly affect the belief, behavior, values, and standards of the society they govern or manage.

Example:

-In Saudi Arabia, a man is in charge of a woman's life from birth until she passes away. Every woman must have a male guardian who has the authority to make several important choices on her behalf. Typically, this guardian is a father or husband, but in other situations, it may be a brother or son. Women are essentially treated as perpetual legal adolescents by the Saudi government. The Saudi Arabian government has made very little progress in eradicating the system, which continues to be the most significant barrier to women's rights in the nation.
