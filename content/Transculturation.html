Transculturation

A process of cultural transformation known as transculturation is characterized by the entrance of new cultural components and the loss or modification of preexisting ones. Essentially, it is a type of socialization and, thus, is a continual process of cultural learning and relearning. Transculturation comprises the interactions between cultures through which a person continuously broadens their knowledge of and aptitude for various cultures. It is challenging to identify and credit the source when using this form because it takes components from several civilizations and combines them.
